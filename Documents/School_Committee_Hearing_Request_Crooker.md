## III. GROUNDS FOR CHALLENGE

The student records containing NC/CP designations and any summer school requirement are inaccurate and procedurally invalid for the following reasons:

### Ground 1: Procedural Invalidity

The school imposed a penalty without following the mandatory intervention process required by its own handbook. Under Massachusetts education law and DESE policy, intervention must precede escalation. The school skipped every required step and imposed a penalty at the endpoint. This is procedurally void.

### Ground 2: Policy Self-Contradiction

The handbook cannot be consistently enforced. Page 43 states there is "no differentiation between excused and unexcused absences." Pages 44-45 rely entirely on that distinction. A penalty imposed under a policy that contradicts itself cannot produce an accurate record.

### Ground 3: Legal Thresholds Not Met

| Statute | Threshold | Angelina | Status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| MGL Ch. 119 §21 | 8+ unexcused per quarter | Q1=5, Q2=2, Q3=4, Q4=0 | NOT MET |
| MGL Ch. 76 §2 | 7+ unexcused in 6 months | S1=7, S2=4 | NOT MET |
| DESE 10% Rule | 18 days | 16 days (8.9%) | NOT MET |

The school has treated Angelina as though she meets thresholds that Massachusetts law explicitly states she does not.

### Ground 4: The NC/CP Designation Is Based on Data the School Allowed to Accumulate

The only reason Angelina has 9+ total absences — the NC threshold under Page 45 — is that the school failed to intervene when she had 5 unexcused absences on October 27, 2025. Had the school followed its own policy at that point, the parent would have been notified, an action plan would have been created, and a meeting would have occurred — all of which could have addressed and corrected the attendance pattern before it reached the NC threshold.

The school cannot suppress the intervention and then penalize the student for the consequences of that suppression.
