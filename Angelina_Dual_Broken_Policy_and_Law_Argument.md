# The Argument: Both the Policy and the Law Were Broken
## Angelina Karima Crooker (Grade 9) — Wareham High School
## Neutral, source-anchored

---

## CORE PREMISE

Two distinct legal instruments set the standard of conduct for the school:

1. **The school's own policy** — the Wareham High School Handbook, pp. 42–45.
2. **Massachusetts law** — MGL c. 71, §§ 37H and 37H½; MGL c. 76, §§ 1B and 2; MGL c. 119, § 21; 603 CMR 23.00; DESE chronic absenteeism guidelines; 14th Amendment, U.S. Const.

The school broke both. The penalty stands in violation of each. The penalty cannot rest on either instrument, and therefore cannot rest at all.

---

## PART 1 — THE SCHOOL'S OWN POLICY WAS BROKEN

### What the handbook required (p. 44, verbatim)

> "Upon reaching the fifth unexcused absence in a school year, parents/guardians will be notified. Students will be required to develop an action plan for student's attendance with the Principal or designee, Guidance Counselor and their parents/guardians. The Principal or designee, or Guidance Counselor **shall** make a reasonable effort to meet with the parent or guardian of a student who has 5 or more unexcused absences."

### What actually happened

The student reached her 5th unexcused absence on **October 27, 2025**. The school's PowerSchool system recorded it (Code "A" — Absent Unexcused). The Dean of Students' email of June 23, 2026 confirms the date and code.

From October 27, 2025 through June 23, 2026 — **eight months** — the school did not:

- Notify the parent of the 5th unexcused absence;
- Develop an action plan with the student, the parent, the Principal or designee, and the Guidance Counselor;
- Make a reasonable effort to meet with the parent.

A search of all family Gmail accounts shows zero emails from Wareham High School regarding the student's attendance for the entire school year. The school has three contact persons on file (mother, aunt, grandmother) and has contacted all three on other matters in the last three months.

### The internal contradictions in the handbook (p. 43 vs. p. 44 vs. p. 45)

The school's own policy cannot be reconciled on its face:

- **Page 43** treats absences without differentiating excused from unexcused.
- **Page 44** keys the intervention trigger exclusively to **unexcused** absences.
- **Page 45** keys the No-Credit (NC) designation to **"9 total days absent"** — without the unexcused distinction.

A student can therefore reach the NC trigger (p. 45) without the intervention trigger (p. 44) ever firing. The intervention is, on the handbook's own terms, not an actual safety net for any student whose absences include both excused and unexcused days.

The CP/NC framework (pp. 42–45) contains no provision for tardies. PowerSchool records 27 tardies. The school is using an unwritten standard.

### Conclusion on the policy

The handbook required the school to engage the family at 5 unexcused absences. The school did not engage. The handbook is internally contradictory. The penalty was imposed under a policy the school did not follow and could not, on its own terms, support.

## PART 2 — MASSACHUSETTS LAW WAS BROKEN

### A. MGL c. 76, § 1B (notification and action-step mandate)

The statute requires every school committee to have a policy that includes:

> "a requirement that a reasonable effort be made to notify the parent or guardian of a child of any absence and to obtain from the parent or guardian an explanation for the absence; ... a requirement that, where a child is repeatedly absent from school, a reasonable effort be made to arrange a meeting between the parent or guardian and school officials to discuss the reasons for the absences and to develop a plan to improve the child's attendance; and ... a requirement that, where a child is habitually absent or truant, a reasonable effort be made by school officials to meet with the parent or guardian of the child to develop a plan to improve the child's attendance."

Each component uses the mandatory term "shall." The school performed none of them.

### B. MGL c. 71, § 37H (handbook and due process)

The statute provides:

> "Each school district's policies pertaining to the conduct of students **shall** include the following: disciplinary proceedings, including procedures assuring due process; standards and procedures for suspension and expulsion of students ..."

The credit-withholding designation is a disciplinary measure under § 37H. It requires the handbook's due-process procedures to be followed. The handbook's own due-process procedures were not followed.

§ 37H further provides:

> "In each school building containing the grades nine to twelve, inclusive, the principal, in consultation with the school council, **shall** prepare and distribute to each student a student handbook setting forth the rules pertaining to the conduct of students."

> "The school council **shall** review the student handbook each spring to consider changes in disciplinary policy to take effect in September of the following school year."

The contradictions identified in Part 1 indicate that the required spring review either did not occur or did not catch the contradictions. Either way, the statutory annual-review duty was not satisfied.

### C. MGL c. 71, § 37H½ (procedural template for any student-facing removal)

The statute establishes the procedural floor for any student-facing removal:

- "The student **shall** receive written notification of the charges and the reasons for such suspension prior to such suspension taking effect."
- "The student **shall** also receive written notification of his right to appeal and the process for appealing such suspension."
- "The student **shall** have the right to appeal the suspension to the superintendent."
- "The student **shall** notify the superintendent in writing of his request for an appeal no later than **five calendar days** following the effective date of the suspension."
- "The superintendent **shall** hold a hearing with the student and the student's parent or guardian within **three calendar days** of the student's request for an appeal."
- "At the hearing, the student **shall** have the right to present oral and written testimony on his behalf, and **shall** have the right to counsel."
- "The superintendent **shall** render a decision on the appeal within **five calendar days** of the hearing."

| § 37H½ protection | What the school did |
|---|---|
| Written notice of charges and reasons prior to action | None — first written contact was the penalty itself |
| Written notice of right to appeal and appeal process | None provided |
| Five-day window to request appeal | No notice of any window |
| Three-day hearing before superintendent | Not held |
| Right to present oral and written testimony | Not afforded |
| Right to counsel | Not afforded |
| Superintendent's written decision within five days | Not issued |

The school performed **none** of the seven § 37H½ protections. § 37H½ by its terms sets the standard for any student-facing removal; the school did not meet that standard.

### D. MGL c. 76, § 2 (parental liability threshold)

The statute sets the parental liability threshold at **7 or more unexcused full school days in a six-month period**. The student's numbers: Semester 1 = 7 unexcused (5 Q1 + 2 Q2) — exactly at the threshold; Semester 2 = 4 unexcused. The student does not clearly and consistently exceed the statutory threshold. The school cannot premise a penalty on a parental-liability theory.

### E. MGL c. 119, § 21 (habitual truancy)

The statute defines a "habitual truant" as a student who willfully fails to attend school for **more than 8 school days in a quarter**. The student's quarterly unexcused totals: Q1 = 5; Q2 = 2; Q3 = 4; Q4 = 0. **The student has not met the habitual-truancy threshold in any quarter.** The school has no statutory authority to file a CRA petition under MGL c. 119, § 39E.

### F. DESE chronic absenteeism guideline (10% rule)

DESE defines chronic absenteeism as **10% or more of all school days**. With 180 school days, the threshold is 18 days. The student has 16 total absences (8.9%). **The student is below the DESE chronic absenteeism threshold.**

### G. 603 CMR 23.00 (student records)

The regulations grant parents the right to challenge inaccurate, misleading, or procedurally invalid information in a student's record (603 CMR 23.07, 23.09). A credit-withholding designation imposed without the § 1B, § 37H, and § 37H½ procedures the law requires is, by definition, procedurally invalid information in the student's record. The parent has invoked the right to challenge.

### H. 14th Amendment, U.S. Constitution

> "Nor shall any State deprive any person of life, liberty, or property, without due process of law."

A credit designation on a student's transcript is a recognized property interest in educational benefits. *Goss v. Lopez*, 419 U.S. 565 (1975). Minimum process requires notice of the charge and an opportunity to be heard. The school provided neither.

## PART 3 — THE TWO INSTRUMENTS STAND TOGETHER; THE SCHOOL FELL BENEATH BOTH

The handbook is binding on the school. The General Laws are binding on the school. The 14th Amendment is binding on the school. The school fell beneath all three.

The penalty rests on:

- A handbook provision the school did not follow (p. 44);
- A handbook framework the school cannot reconcile on its face (p. 43 / p. 44 / p. 45);
- Statutory intervention duties the school did not perform (c. 76, § 1B);
- Statutory handbook and due-process duties the school did not satisfy (c. 71, § 37H);
- Statutory procedural protections the school did not provide (c. 71, § 37H½);
- A threshold (habitual truancy) the student does not meet (c. 119, § 21);
- A threshold (chronic absenteeism) the student does not meet (DESE);
- A threshold (parental liability) the student does not clearly exceed (c. 76, § 2);
- And a procedural floor (notice and hearing) the school did not honor (14th Amendment).

The policy was broken. The law was broken. The penalty cannot stand against either.

## PART 4 — WHAT MUST HAPPEN

1. Rescind the summer school requirement.
2. Rescind all NC/CP designations and preserve the grades as earned.
3. Provide a § 37H½-compliant notice, appeal opportunity, hearing, and written decision.
4. Reconcile the handbook's internal contradictions.
5. Document the spring school-council review required by § 37H.
6. Bring the handbook into conformity with MGL c. 76, § 1B and MGL c. 71, §§ 37H and 37H½.

The policy was broken. The law was broken. The remedy is to honor both.
