## I. PURPOSE

I am writing to request civil rights assistance from your organization on behalf of my daughter, Angelina Karima Crooker, a 9th-grade IEP student at Wareham High School. The school district has imposed a summer school requirement and NC/CP transcript designations for absences that are disability-related, without engaging the procedural protections required by M.G.L. c. 71B, the IDEA, Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act, Title II of the ADA, M.G.L. c. 71, § 37H½, and the procedural due process clause of the 14th Amendment.

## II. SUMMARY OF CONCERNS

The principal concerns are:

1. **Failure to engage the Section 504 interactive process** despite the school having constructive notice of disability-related barriers (bullying, late-day concentration of absences, academic-against-attendance divergence).

2. **Failure to convene the IEP team** before imposing a significant change in placement (loss of credit, mandatory summer school, possible retention).

3. **Failure to conduct a manifestation determination review** under 34 CFR § 300.530.

4. **Failure to provide procedural safeguards notices** under 34 CFR § 104.36 and M.G.L. c. 71B, § 3.

5. **Failure to engage the graduated procedural framework** of M.G.L. c. 76, § 1B (notification, action plan, parent meeting) before imposing the most serious consequences.

6. **Failure to provide M.G.L. c. 71, § 37H½ hearing** (written notice, opportunity for hearing, right to counsel, right to present evidence, written decision) before imposing a penalty that affects the permanent transcript.

7. **Internal contradiction** in the school attendance policy — simultaneously states "no differentiation between excused and unexcused absences" while relying entirely on the distinction.

8. **14-day discrepancy** in the attendance record (PowerSchool 16 vs. school claim 30) that the school has been unable or unwilling to explain.

## III. ASSISTANCE REQUESTED

I am requesting the following assistance:

1. **A review of the matter** to determine whether the facts and law warrant organizational involvement.

2. **Co-counsel or referral** to pro bono counsel with expertise in special education, disability rights, and/or education law.

3. **Investigation** of the school district's compliance with M.G.L. c. 71B, the IDEA, Section 504, and Title II.

4. **Public advocacy** as the matter presents a structural concern about how school districts across the Commonwealth are applying (or failing to apply) the procedural framework of M.G.L. c. 76, § 1B and M.G.L. c. 71, § 37H and § 37H½ to attendance-driven credit penalties imposed on students with disabilities.
