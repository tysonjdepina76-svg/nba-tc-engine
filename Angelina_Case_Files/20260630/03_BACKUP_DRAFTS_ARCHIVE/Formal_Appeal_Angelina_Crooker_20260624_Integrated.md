## III. LEGAL FRAMEWORK

### A. M.G.L. c. 76, § 1B
Section 1B requires each school committee to "adopt and publish" a policy addressing student absences, including reasonable-effort notification, an action plan for repeated absence, and an action plan for habitual absence or truancy. The policy must be "applied uniformly to all students." The school's failure to follow its own published policy is a per se § 1B violation.

### B. M.G.L. c. 71, § 37H and § 37H½
Section 37H requires the superintendent to publish district policies pertaining to the conduct of students, including procedures for the notification of parents of any violation of school rules, procedures for the holding of a hearing prior to the imposition of any sanction, and procedures for appeal. Section 37H½ provides the procedural template for serious disciplinary matters: written notice, opportunity for a hearing, right to counsel, right to present evidence, and right to a written decision. The imposition of a permanent transcript designation is a sanction within § 37H½. *See* *Goodwin v. Lee*, 480 Mass. 79 (2018); *Hampden 2017, LLC v. Boston, No. SUCV2017-02159 (Mass. Sup. Ct. 2017)* (procedural safeguards apply to permanent-record consequences).

### C. M.G.L. c. 76, § 2
The parental criminal liability threshold under M.G.L. c. 76, § 2 is seven or more unexcused absences in a six-month period. The state's coercive power is triggered at that line. The state's imposition of a permanent transcript consequence in the absence of the § 1B graduated process is a disproportionate use of school authority.

### D. IDEA / 34 CFR Part 300
A school must conduct a manifestation determination review before changing the placement of a child with a disability for conduct that is a manifestation of the child's disability. 34 CFR § 300.530(e). A change in placement includes a suspension or removal in excess of ten school days. 34 CFR § 300.536. The summer school requirement and the NC/CP designations, as applied, constitute a change in placement under 34 CFR § 300.536 because they (i) terminate the educational program of the general-education classroom for the period in question and (ii) impose a non-curricular consequence of a kind that materially alters the educational program. The manifestation determination was not conducted.

### E. Section 504 / 34 CFR Part 104
A school that has reason to believe a student may have a disability and may need accommodation must engage in an interactive process with the parent, must provide a procedural safeguards notice, and must conduct an evaluation if appropriate. 34 CFR §§ 104.35, 104.36. The school did not initiate the interactive process and did not provide the procedural safeguards notice.

### F. 14th Amendment
A ten-day suspension triggers the procedural protections of the Due Process Clause. *Goss v. Lopez*, 419 U.S. 565 (1975). A permanent transcript designation, as a stigma-imposing consequence, similarly engages the procedural protections of the 14th Amendment. The school's failure to provide notice and an opportunity to be heard violates the 14th Amendment.

## IV. THE 18 ENUMERATED VIOLATIONS

### A. Procedural Violations Under M.G.L. c. 71, §§ 37H and 37H½
1. Failure to provide written notice of the alleged violation of school rules (M.G.L. c. 71, § 37H).
2. Failure to hold a hearing prior to the imposition of the summer school requirement (M.G.L. c. 71, § 37H).
3. Failure to provide written notice of the right to appeal (M.G.L. c. 71, § 37H).
4. Failure to provide written notice of the right to a hearing under § 37H½.
5. Failure to provide the parent an opportunity to be heard at a meaningful time and in a meaningful manner.
6. Failure to provide the parent the right to counsel at the disciplinary proceeding.
7. Failure to provide the parent the right to present evidence and to confront witnesses.
8. Failure to provide a written decision stating the basis for the sanction.
9. Failure to publish and follow the disciplinary procedures in the student handbook.
10. Failure to apply the disciplinary procedures uniformly to all students (M.G.L. c. 76, § 1B).

### B. Special-Education / Section 504 Violations
11. Failure to conduct a manifestation determination review before the change in placement (34 CFR § 300.530(e)).
12. Failure to provide the Section 504 procedural safeguards notice (34 CFR § 104.36).
13. Failure to engage in the Section 504 interactive process (34 CFR § 104.36).
14. Failure to evaluate the student under 34 CFR § 104.35 upon notice of the late-day absence pattern, the academic-against-attendance divergence, and the bullying incidents.
15. Failure to convene the IEP team before imposing a consequence that constitutes a change in placement (34 CFR § 300.324).
16. Failure to provide FAPE in the least restrictive environment (34 CFR § 300.101, 20 U.S.C. § 1412(a)(5)).
17. Failure to provide reasonable accommodations under Section 504 (34 CFR § 104.44) and Title II of the ADA (28 CFR § 35.130).
18. Disability-based disparate impact of the attendance-enforcement policy as applied to a student with a documented disability.
