## II. IEP STATUS

Angelina Karima Crooker is a 9th-grade student at Wareham High School with an active Individualized Education Program (IEP). The IEP was developed and is maintained by the school district. The IEP was in effect throughout the period of the conduct complained of in this matter (October 27, 2025 to June 23, 2026).

## III. ADDITIONAL VIOLATIONS

### Violation 19: Failure to Convene the IEP Team

Under 34 CFR § 300.324, the school district must ensure that the IEP team is convened to review and revise the student's IEP, as appropriate, to address any lack of expected progress toward the annual goals and in the general curriculum. The school district's pattern of attendance-related concerns — eleven unexcused absences — was a clear signal that the IEP team should have been convened. The school district did not convene the IEP team. The school district did not review the attendance pattern. The school district did not determine whether the pattern was a manifestation of the student's disability. The school district did not adjust the student's IEP in light of the pattern.

### Violation 20: Failure to Conduct a Manifestation Determination Review

Under 34 CFR § 300.530, the school district must conduct a manifestation determination review within ten school days of any decision to change the placement of a child with a disability because of a violation of a code of student conduct. The "change of placement" includes any removal that exceeds ten school days. The school district's imposition of the summer school requirement and the NC/CP designations is, in substance, a change of placement. The school district did not conduct a manifestation determination review. The school district did not convene the relevant members of the IEP team to determine whether the conduct (here, the absences) was a manifestation of the student's disability. The school district did not make the determination that the IDEA requires.

### Violation 21: Failure to Provide the Section 504 Procedural Safeguards Notice

Under 34 CFR § 104.36, a school district must provide a procedural safeguards notice to parents of students who are receiving Section 504 services or who are the subject of a Section 504 referral. The procedural safeguards notice includes the parent's right to request a hearing, the right to participate in the hearing, the right to be represented by counsel, and the right to review the student's educational record. The school district did not provide the Section 504 procedural safeguards notice. The school district did not initiate a Section 504 referral. The school district did not engage in the Section 504 interactive process.

## IV. RELIEF REQUESTED

The undersigned respectfully requests that the school district, in addition to the relief requested in the underlying documents:

1. **Convene the IEP team** under 34 CFR § 300.324 to review the student's IEP in light of the attendance pattern and the pattern of disability-related concerns. The IEP team should determine whether the pattern is a manifestation of the student's disability and, if so, what accommodations are appropriate.

2. **Conduct a manifestation determination review** under 34 CFR § 300.530 to determine whether the absences were a manifestation of the student's disability.

3. **Provide the Section 504 procedural safeguards notice** under 34 CFR § 104.36 to the parent.

4. **Initiate the Section 504 interactive process** under 34 CFR § 104.36 to determine whether the student needs Section 504 accommodations to address the disability-related experiences that contributed to the attendance pattern.

5. **Rescind the summer school requirement and the NC/CP designations** pending the IEP team review and the manifestation determination review.
