## I. JURISDICTION

This complaint is filed with the Massachusetts Department of Elementary and Secondary Education Problem Resolution System ("PRS") under 34 CFR § 300.151–.153 and 603 CMR 28.0900. The PRS has jurisdiction over complaints alleging violations of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act ("IDEA"), 20 U.S.C. § 1400 et seq., and the Massachusetts special-education regulations, 603 CMR 28.00.

## II. THE STUDENT
Angelina Karima Crooker is a 9th-grade student at Wareham High School (7 Viking Drive, Wareham, MA 02571). She is a child with a disability as defined by 34 CFR § 300.8 and an active IEP. She is also a qualified individual with a disability under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973, 29 U.S.C. § 794.

## III. THE 18 VIOLATIONS

The Wareham Public Schools have committed the following eighteen (18) violations of the IDEA, Section 504, M.G.L. c. 71B, 603 CMR 28.00, M.G.L. c. 71 §§ 37H and 37H½, and M.G.L. c. 76 §§ 1, 1B, and 2:

**M.G.L. c. 71, §§ 37H and 37H½:**
1. Failure to provide written notice of the alleged violation of school rules.
2. Failure to hold a hearing prior to the imposition of the summer school requirement.
3. Failure to provide written notice of the right to appeal.
4. Failure to provide written notice of the right to a hearing under § 37H½.
5. Failure to provide the parent an opportunity to be heard at a meaningful time and in a meaningful manner.
6. Failure to provide the parent the right to counsel.
7. Failure to provide the parent the right to present evidence and to confront witnesses.
8. Failure to provide a written decision stating the basis for the sanction.
9. Failure to publish and follow the disciplinary procedures in the student handbook.
10. Failure to apply the disciplinary procedures uniformly to all students.

**IDEA / Section 504 / 14th Amendment:**
11. Failure to conduct a manifestation determination review before the change in placement.
12. Failure to provide the Section 504 procedural safeguards notice.
13. Failure to engage in the Section 504 interactive process.
14. Failure to evaluate the student under 34 CFR § 104.35.
15. Failure to convene the IEP team before imposing a change in placement.
16. Failure to provide FAPE in the LRE.
17. Failure to provide reasonable accommodations under Section 504 and the ADA.
18. Disability-based disparate impact of the attendance-enforcement policy.

## IV. STATEMENT OF FACTS

1. Angelina is an Honor Roll student with a B+ average in her core academic courses.
2. She has 16 total absences (11 unexcused, 5 excused) per PowerSchool. The school has asserted a "30 absences" figure that cannot be reconciled with the PowerSchool record; the 14-day discrepancy has not been explained.
3. On October 27, 2025, she reached her fifth unexcused absence. Under Page 44 of the Wareham High School Student Handbook, three mandatory actions were required of the school: parent notification, creation of an action plan, and a reasonable effort by the school to meet with the parent. None of the three actions was taken.
4. On June 23, 2026, in the final week of the school year, the school imposed a summer school requirement and NC/CP designations on her transcript.
5. The school did not provide a Section 504 procedural safeguards notice.
6. The school did not convene the IEP team to determine whether the absences were a manifestation of disability.
7. The school did not provide the pre-removal hearing required by M.G.L. c. 71, § 37H½.

## V. RELIEF REQUESTED

The undersigned respectfully requests that PRS:

1. Investigate the 18 enumerated violations.
2. Order rescission of the summer school requirement and removal of the NC/CP designations.
3. Order the district to conduct a manifestation determination review.
4. Order the district to convene the IEP team within ten (10) school days.
5. Order the district to initiate the Section 504 interactive process and provide the procedural safeguards notice.
6. Order compensatory education and/or services as appropriate.
7. Order the district to provide training to its staff on the procedural protections of M.G.L. c. 71, §§ 37H and 37H½, and on the IDEA's manifestation determination requirements.
8. Provide any other corrective action that PRS deems appropriate.
