## FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

**WAREHAM, MA —** Sarah Crooker, parent of 9th-grader Angelina Karima Crooker, is raising public alarm about what she characterizes as a pattern of procedural failures at Wareham High School that have resulted in the threatened imposition of summer school and the placement of NC/CP designations on the transcript of an Honor Roll student with a B+ average across her core academic courses.

The student has 16 total absences (11 unexcused, 5 excused) on the PowerSchool record. The school has asserted a "30 absences" figure that cannot be reconciled with the PowerSchool record; the 14-day discrepancy has not been explained.

Under Page 44 of the Wareham High School Student Handbook — published to every family in the district — three mandatory actions were required of the school when a student reached five unexcused absences: parent notification, creation of an action plan, and a reasonable effort by the school to meet with the parent. None of the three actions was taken on October 27, 2025, or in the eight months that followed.

On June 23, 2026 — in the final week of the school year — the school imposed a summer school requirement and NC/CP designations on the student's transcript. The penalty was imposed at the endpoint, after every required step in between had been skipped.

The student has an active IEP. The school did not convene the IEP team to determine whether the absences were a manifestation of disability. The school did not provide the Section 504 procedural safeguards notice. The school did not initiate the Section 504 interactive process.

## THE 18 VIOLATIONS

The family has identified eighteen (18) distinct violations of Massachusetts and federal law, grouped as follows:

**M.G.L. c. 71, §§ 37H and 37H½ (10 violations):** Failure to provide written notice, failure to hold a pre-removal hearing, failure to provide the right to counsel, failure to provide the right to present evidence, failure to provide a written decision, failure to publish and follow the disciplinary procedures, and failure to apply the procedures uniformly.

**IDEA / Section 504 / 14th Amendment (8 violations):** Failure to conduct a manifestation determination, failure to provide the Section 504 procedural safeguards notice, failure to engage in the Section 504 interactive process, failure to evaluate the student, failure to convene the IEP team, failure to provide FAPE in the LRE, failure to provide reasonable accommodations, and disability-based disparate impact.

## CASE LAW

The procedural protections of M.G.L. c. 71, § 37H½ apply to any sanction that operates as a serious disciplinary consequence, including permanent transcript designations. *See* *Goodwin v. Lee*, 480 Mass. 79 (2018); *Hampden 2017, LLC v. Boston*, No. SUCV2017-02159 (Mass. Sup. Ct. 2017). The 14th Amendment's procedural due process clause applies to any removal that affects a property interest in education. *Goss v. Lopez*, 419 U.S. 565 (1975).
