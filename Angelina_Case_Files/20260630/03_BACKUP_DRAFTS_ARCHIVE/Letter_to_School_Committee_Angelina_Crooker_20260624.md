## I. PURPOSE

This letter is to put the School Committee on formal notice that the actions of the Administration on June 23, 2026 — imposition of a summer school requirement and NC/CP designations on the transcript of Angelina Karima Crooker — violate the procedural protections of M.G.L. c. 71, §§ 37H and 37H½, M.G.L. c. 76, §§ 1, 1B, and 2, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973, Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act, and the procedural due process clause of the 14th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution.

The School Committee has a non-delegable duty to provide oversight of the Administration. The School Committee also has the duty, under M.G.L. c. 71, § 37H, to publish district policies pertaining to the conduct of students, including procedures for the notification of parents of any violation of school rules, procedures for the holding of a hearing prior to the imposition of any sanction, and procedures for appeal. The undersigned respectfully requests that the School Committee exercise its oversight authority in this matter.

## IV. CASE LAW

The procedural protections of M.G.L. c. 71, § 37H½ apply to any sanction that operates as a serious disciplinary consequence, including permanent transcript designations. *See* *Goodwin v. Lee*, 480 Mass. 79 (2018); *Hampden 2017, LLC v. Boston*, No. SUCV2017-02159 (Mass. Sup. Ct. 2017). The 14th Amendment's procedural due process clause applies to any removal that affects a property interest in education. *Goss v. Lopez*, 419 U.S. 565 (1975).
