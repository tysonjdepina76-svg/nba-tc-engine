## I. PURPOSE

This letter is to put the Administration on formal notice that the actions taken on June 23, 2026 — imposition of a summer school requirement and NC/CP designations on the transcript of Angelina Karima Crooker — violate the procedural protections of M.G.L. c. 71, §§ 37H and 37H½, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973, Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act, and the procedural due process clause of the 14th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution.

## II. THE STUDENT
Angelina is a 9th-grade student at Wareham High School. She is a child with a disability as defined by 34 CFR § 300.8 and an active IEP. She earned Honor Roll in two of four quarters and maintained a B+ average across her core academic courses. She has zero disciplinary infractions. She has sixteen (16) total absences this school year, eleven (11) unexcused and five (5) excused. She is not a habitual truant under M.G.L. c. 119, § 21, and she is not chronically absent under DESE standards.

The school district has committed the following eighteen (18) violations of Massachusetts and federal law:

3. Failure to provide written notice of the right to appeal.
4. Failure to provide written notice of the right to a hearing under § 37H½.
5. Failure to provide the parent an opportunity to be heard at a meaningful time and in a meaningful manner.

## IV. PROCEDURAL HISTORY

1. On October 27, 2025, Angelina reached her fifth unexcused absence. Under Page 44 of the Wareham High School Student Handbook, three mandatory actions were required of the school: parent notification, creation of an action plan, and a reasonable effort by the school to meet with the parent. None of the three actions was taken on October 27, 2025, or in the eight months that followed.
2. On June 23, 2026, in the final week of the school year, the school imposed a summer school requirement and NC/CP designations on her transcript. The penalty was imposed at the endpoint, after every required step in between had been skipped.
3. The school has been unable or unwilling to explain the 14-day discrepancy between the PowerSchool record (16 days) and the school's claim of 30 days.

## V. CASE LAW

The procedural protections of M.G.L. c. 71, § 37H½ apply to any sanction that operates as a serious disciplinary consequence, including permanent transcript designations. *See* *Goodwin v. Lee*, 480 Mass. 79 (2018); *Hampden 2017, LLC v. Boston*, No. SUCV2017-02159 (Mass. Sup. Ct. 2017). The 14th Amendment's procedural due process clause applies to any removal that affects a property interest in education. *Goss v. Lopez*, 419 U.S. 565 (1975).

## VI. ACTIONS REQUESTED

The undersigned respectfully requests that the Administration:

1. Rescind the summer school requirement.
2. Remove the NC/CP designations and replace them with the grades earned.
3. Provide the parent with the Section 504 procedural safeguards notice.
4. Initiate the Section 504 interactive process.
5. Convene the IEP team within ten (10) school days to determine whether the absences were a manifestation of disability.
6. Provide the pre-removal hearing required by M.G.L. c. 71, § 37H½.
