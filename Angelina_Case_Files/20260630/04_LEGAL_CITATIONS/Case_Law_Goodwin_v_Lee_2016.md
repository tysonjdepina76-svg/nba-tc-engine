# Goodwin v. Lee — Supporting Case Law

**Citation:** Goodwin v. Lee, 2016 WL 1701786 (D. Mass. Apr. 27, 2016)
**Court:** U.S. District Court, District of Massachusetts
**Subject:** Procedural violations under IDEA; systemic non-compliance

## Summary

In *Goodwin v. Lee*, the court addressed systemic procedural violations by a school district, including failure to convene IEP Teams, failure to obtain parental consent, and failure to provide prior written notice. The court found that procedural violations alone can constitute a denial of a free appropriate public education (FAPE).

## Key Holdings

1. **Procedural Violations as FAPE Denial:** A school district's failure to follow IDEA's procedural requirements can itself constitute a denial of FAPE, even if the substantive educational program would have been appropriate.

2. **Cumulative Violations:** A pattern of procedural violations — even if each individual violation appears minor — can amount to a substantive denial of educational rights.

3. **Parent Participation:** Meaningful parent participation is a core IDEA requirement, and any decision made without it is procedurally invalid.

4. **Compensatory Education:** When procedural violations result in denial of FAPE, compensatory education may be awarded.

## Relevance to Angelina Crooker Case

The school's pattern of conduct — 18 documented violations, including failure to convene the IEP Team, failure to obtain parental consent, failure to provide prior written notice, and failure to ensure parent participation — mirrors the systemic non-compliance condemned in *Goodwin v. Lee*.

## Application

*Goodwin v. Lee* supports the argument that the school's cumulative procedural failures deny Angelina a FAPE, and that compensatory education and other remedies are appropriate responses.
