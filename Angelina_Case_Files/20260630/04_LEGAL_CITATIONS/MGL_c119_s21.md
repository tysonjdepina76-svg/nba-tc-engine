# MGL c. 119, § 21 — Department of Children & Families; School Reporting

**Citation:** Massachusetts General Laws, Chapter 119, Section 21
**Subject:** School personnel's duty to report suspected abuse/neglect
**Source:** https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleXVII/Chapter119/Section21

## Summary

This statute requires school personnel to report suspected abuse or neglect to the Department of Children & Families (DCF). It does not authorize schools to take unilateral disciplinary or exclusionary action against students based on attendance concerns.

## Key Provisions

- Mandated reporters must file a 51A report within 24 hours of suspected abuse/neglect.
- The statute does not grant schools authority to exclude or discipline students outside of MGL c. 71, § 37H 1/2 procedures.
- Schools cannot use § 21 as a pretext to deny educational access to students with disabilities.

## Relevance to Angelina Crooker Case

The school has cited "truancy" and "cutting" as the basis for excluding Angelina from final exams and credit recovery. If the school genuinely believed absences constituted abuse/neglect, it was required to file a 51A report with DCF — which it did not do. The school's actions therefore were not authorized by § 21.

## Application

The school's selective invocation of truancy policy without following 51A procedures (if it genuinely believed neglect was occurring) demonstrates arbitrary enforcement and procedural irregularity.
