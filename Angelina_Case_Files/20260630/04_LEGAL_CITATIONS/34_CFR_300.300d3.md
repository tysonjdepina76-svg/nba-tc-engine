# 34 CFR § 300.300(d)(3) — Parental Consent for Reevaluation

**Citation:** 34 Code of Federal Regulations, Section 300.300(d)(3)
**Subject:** Parental consent for reevaluation
**Source:** https://sites.ed.gov/idea/regs/b/d/300.300

## Summary

This federal regulation requires informed parental consent before a school district may conduct a reevaluation of a student with a disability.

## Key Provisions

- The school must obtain informed parental consent before conducting any reevaluation.
- If the parent refuses to consent, the school may, but is not required to, pursue the reevaluation through due process.
- The school may not override parental refusal through unilateral action.
- Reevaluation without consent constitutes a procedural violation that may be challenged through a due process hearing or state complaint.

## Relevance to Angelina Crooker Case

If the school conducted or attempted to conduct any reevaluation of Angelina without Sarah Crooker's written consent, it violated this regulation. Additionally, the school cannot use any "reevaluation" as a basis for changing Angelina's placement without proper consent.

## Application

Sarah Crooker has not provided written consent for any reevaluation. Any school action based on a purported reevaluation is procedurally invalid and subject to challenge.
