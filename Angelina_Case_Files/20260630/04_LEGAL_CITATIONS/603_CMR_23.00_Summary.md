# 603 CMR 23.00 — Student Records Regulations

**Citation:** 603 Code of Massachusetts Regulations, Section 23.00
**Subject:** Student records access, amendment, and confidentiality
**Source:** https://www.doe.mass.edu/lawsregs/603cmr23.html

## Summary

These regulations govern the collection, maintenance, disclosure, and amendment of student records by Massachusetts public schools.

## Key Provisions

- **Right of Access:** Parents and eligible students (18+) have the right to inspect and review all student records within 10 days of a written request.
- **Records Request Response:** Schools must respond to records requests within 10 business days.
- **Records Challenge:** Parents may challenge the accuracy or completeness of records through a formal amendment process.
- **Disclosure Limits:** Schools may not disclose student records to third parties without parental consent, except as specifically authorized.
- **Destruction of Records:** Schools must provide notice before destroying any student records.

## Relevance to Angelina Crooker Case

Sarah Crooker has a pending records request. The school is required to:
1. Respond within 10 business days.
2. Provide complete copies of all records.
3. Allow the parent to challenge any inaccurate or incomplete records.

## Application

If the school fails to respond or provides incomplete records, it is in violation of 603 CMR 23.00. Any subsequent disciplinary or academic decisions based on incomplete records would be procedurally invalid.
