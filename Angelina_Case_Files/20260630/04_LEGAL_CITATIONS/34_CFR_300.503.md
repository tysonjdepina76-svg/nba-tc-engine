# 34 CFR § 300.503 — Prior Written Notice by the School District

**Citation:** 34 Code of Federal Regulations, Section 300.503
**Subject:** Required prior written notice before school actions
**Source:** https://sites.ed.gov/idea/regs/b/d/300.503

## Summary

This federal regulation requires the school district to provide parents with prior written notice before taking certain actions affecting the student's educational program.

## Key Provisions

The school must provide written notice at least 10 business days before:
- Proposing or refusing to initiate a change in the student's identification, evaluation, or educational placement.
- Proposing or refusing any change to the student's IEP or placement.
- Requesting consent for an action that requires parental consent.

The notice must include:
1. A description of the action proposed or refused.
2. An explanation of why the action is being proposed or refused.
3. A description of each evaluation, assessment, or report used as a basis for the decision.
4. A statement of procedural safeguards available to the parent.
5. Sources of additional assistance.

## Relevance to Angelina Crooker Case

The school made multiple decisions affecting Angelina without providing any prior written notice:
- Excluding her from final exams.
- Denying her credit recovery access.
- Excluding her from summer programming.
- Labeling her absences as "cutting" without medical/disability review.

## Application

Each of these actions required prior written notice with all required components. The school's failure to provide notice is a standalone IDEA violation, separate from its failure to convene the IEP Team.
