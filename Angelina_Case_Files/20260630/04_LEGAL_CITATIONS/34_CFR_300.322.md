# 34 CFR § 300.322 — Parent Participation in IEP Meetings

**Citation:** 34 Code of Federal Regulations, Section 300.322
**Subject:** Ensuring meaningful parent participation in IEP Team decisions
**Source:** https://sites.ed.gov/idea/regs/b/d/300.322

## Summary

This federal regulation requires school districts to ensure that parents have the opportunity to participate meaningfully in all IEP Team meetings.

## Key Provisions

- The school district must take steps to ensure that one or both parents are present at each IEP Team meeting.
- The district must provide parents with adequate notice of meetings.
- Meetings must be scheduled at a mutually agreed-upon time and place.
- The district must use other methods to ensure parent participation if neither parent can attend (telephone, video conference, etc.).
- A meeting may be conducted without a parent only if the district has made and documented multiple attempts to secure participation.

## Relevance to Angelina Crooker Case

The school made 18+ decisions affecting Angelina's education without:

3. Documenting attempts to secure parent participation.
4. Providing alternative means of participation (phone, video).
