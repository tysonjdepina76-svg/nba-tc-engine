# 603 CMR 28.00 — Special Education Regulations

**Citation:** 603 Code of Massachusetts Regulations, Section 28.00
**Subject:** Special education procedures, IEP requirements, and timelines
**Source:** https://www.doe.mass.edu/lawsregs/603cmr28.html

## Summary

These regulations implement the federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) in Massachusetts, governing the identification, evaluation, and education of students with disabilities.

## Key Provisions

- **IEP Team Composition:** The IEP Team must include the parent, the student (when appropriate), regular and special education teachers, a school district representative, and other relevant personnel.
- **IEP Development Timeline:** An IEP must be developed within 30 calendar days of a finding of eligibility.
- **Annual Review:** IEPs must be reviewed at least annually.
- **Prior Written Notice:** Schools must provide prior written notice before changing a student's identification, evaluation, or educational placement.
- **Parental Consent:** Schools must obtain informed parental consent before conducting evaluations, providing services, or making placement changes.

## Relevance to Angelina Crooker Case

Angelina is a student with a documented disability. The school was required to:
1. Convene the IEP Team before making any decisions affecting her placement or services.
2. Obtain parental consent before any changes to her educational program.
3. Provide prior written notice of any proposed actions.

## Application

The school's failure to convene the IEP Team, obtain parental consent, or provide prior written notice before excluding Angelina from final exams and credit recovery constitutes a clear violation of 603 CMR 28.00.
