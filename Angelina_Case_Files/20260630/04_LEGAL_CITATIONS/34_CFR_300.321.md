# 34 CFR § 300.321 — IEP Team Composition and Attendance

**Citation:** 34 Code of Federal Regulations, Section 300.321
**Subject:** Required members of the IEP Team
**Source:** https://sites.ed.gov/idea/regs/b/d/300.321

## Summary

This federal regulation specifies the required members of a student's IEP Team and the procedures for excusing members from Team meetings.

## Key Provisions

**Required IEP Team Members:**
- The parents of the child
- Not less than one regular education teacher of the child
- Not less than one special education teacher of the child
- A school district representative (qualified to provide or supervise specially designed instruction)
- An individual who can interpret the instructional implications of evaluation results
- At the discretion of the parent or district, other individuals with knowledge or expertise
- The child, when appropriate

**Excusal Requirements:**
- A required member may be excused from attending all or part of a meeting if the parent and district agree in writing.
- A member whose area of the curriculum or related services is not being modified may be excused with written parent consent.

## Relevance to Angelina Crooker Case

The school made decisions affecting Angelina's placement and educational program without convening her IEP Team. The parent (Sarah Crooker) was neither notified nor included in any Team meeting, and no written excusal was obtained.

## Application

Every decision the school made regarding Angelina's absences, exclusion from final exams, denial of credit recovery, and exclusion from summer programming required IEP Team action. None of these decisions were made properly.
