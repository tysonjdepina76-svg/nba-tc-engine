# 34 CFR § 300.501 — Independent Educational Evaluations

**Citation:** 34 Code of Federal Regulations, Section 300.501
**Subject:** Parent right to obtain an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE)
**Source:** https://sites.ed.gov/idea/regs/b/d/300.501

## Summary

This federal regulation grants parents the right to obtain an Independent Educational Evaluation (IEE) at public expense when they disagree with the school's evaluation.

## Key Provisions

- Parents have the right to an IEE at public expense if they disagree with the school's evaluation.
- The school must either fund the IEE or request a due process hearing to defend its evaluation.
- The school may not impose conditions or timelines that interfere with the parent's right to an IEE.
- IEE results must be considered by the IEP Team in any decision affecting the student.

## Relevance to Angelina Crooker Case

Sarah Crooker has the right to obtain an IEE at public expense if she disagrees with the school's evaluation of Angelina. The school's failure to convene the IEP Team and consider all available evaluations is a violation of this regulation.

## Application
