# MGL c. 71, § 37H 1/2 — School Discipline and Attendance

**Citation:** Massachusetts General Laws, Chapter 71, Section 37H 1/2
**Subject:** School discipline, suspension, and absence policies
**Source:** https://malegislature.gov/Laws/GeneralLaws/PartI/TitleII/Chapter71/Section37H1-2

## Summary

This statute governs school discipline and student absence policies in Massachusetts, including requirements for due process before any long-term suspension, expulsion, or exclusion from school programming.

## Key Provisions

- A student may be suspended or expelled only after due process procedures are followed.
- The school district must provide written notice of charges and an opportunity for the student to respond.
- For absences deemed "chronic," the district must engage with the parent/guardian before any truancy determination.
- A student cannot be disciplined for absences that are related to a disability without first convening the IEP Team.

## Relevance to Angelina Crooker Case

The school's failure to engage with Sarah Crooker before declaring absences as "cutting" violates due process. Additionally, discipline imposed without IEP Team consideration violates IDEA's protections.
