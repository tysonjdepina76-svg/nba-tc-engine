# Letter to Superintendent D'Andrea

**Date:** July 1, 2026
**From:** Sarah Crooker, parent
**To:** Matthew D'Andrea, Superintendent, Wareham Public Schools
**Re:** Formal Complaint Regarding Procedural Violations at Wareham High School — Angelina Crooker

---

Dear Superintendent D'Andrea,

I am writing to bring to your attention a serious matter involving multiple procedural violations committed by Wareham High School against my daughter, Angelina Crooker, a Grade 9 student with a documented disability.

I have attempted to resolve this matter directly with Principal Palladino, but the school has refused to engage meaningfully or to follow required procedures. I am now escalating this matter to your office.

## Summary of the Situation

Beginning in late May 2026, Angelina experienced an acute medical condition that required ongoing treatment. All absences in June 2026 (16-18 days) were medically necessary, documented, and communicated to the school in advance.

Despite this, the school has:

1. Denied Angelina the opportunity to take final exams
2. Denied Angelina enrollment in summer credit recovery
3. Denied Angelina participation in summer school programming
4. Refused to convene an IEP Team meeting
5. Refused to consider whether absences were disability-related
6. Refused to provide prior written notice for any decisions
7. Refused to obtain parental consent for any changes
8. Failed to respond to a records request submitted on June 26, 2026

These actions have denied Angelina her right to a free appropriate public education (FAPE) under IDEA and Massachusetts law.

## Procedural Violations

The school has committed at least 18 distinct procedural violations, including:

### Federal Law (IDEA / 34 CFR Part 300)

- **34 CFR § 300.321** — Failure to convene IEP Team
- **34 CFR § 300.322** — Failure to ensure parent participation
- **34 CFR § 300.503** — Failure to provide prior written notice
- **34 CFR § 300.300** — Failure to obtain parental consent
- **34 CFR § 300.504** — Failure to provide procedural safeguards notice
- **34 CFR § 300.501** — Failure to maintain or provide access to records

### Massachusetts State Law (603 CMR)

- **603 CMR 28.00** — Failure to follow IEP development and review timelines
- **603 CMR 23.00** — Failure to respond to records request within 10 business days

### Massachusetts General Laws

- **MGL c. 71, § 37H 1/2** — Violation of due process requirements
- **MGL c. 76, § 1B** — Denial of equal access to education
- **MGL c. 119, § 21** — Selective enforcement of truancy policy

A complete list of all 18 violations is maintained in the case file and is available upon request.

## Requested Actions

I am requesting that you, as Superintendent, take the following actions:

1. **Order an immediate halt** to all decisions affecting Angelina until an IEP Team meeting is convened.

2. **Direct the school to convene an IEP Team meeting** within 10 business days.

3. **Order the school to provide prior written notice** for any future decisions affecting Angelina.

4. **Order the school to respond to my records request** within 5 business days.

5. **Conduct an internal investigation** into the procedural violations that have occurred.

6. **Provide written confirmation** of the steps the district will take to ensure compliance with IDEA and Massachusetts law.

7. **Provide compensatory education** for the educational opportunities Angelina has been denied.

## Timeline

I am requesting a written response from your office no later than **July 15, 2026**. If I do not receive a substantive response by that date, I will:

1. File a formal complaint with the Massachusetts Department of Elementary and Secondary Education (DESE)
2. File a hearing request with the Bureau of Special Education Appeals (BSEA)
3. Contact the Massachusetts Office of the Child Advocate
4. Seek judicial review of the district's actions

## Conclusion

I am committed to working with the district to resolve this matter in a way that protects Angelina's rights and ensures she receives the educational opportunities to which she is entitled. I trust that you will give this matter your immediate attention.

I am available to meet with you at your earliest convenience. Please contact me to schedule a meeting.

Sincerely,

Parent of Angelina Crooker
