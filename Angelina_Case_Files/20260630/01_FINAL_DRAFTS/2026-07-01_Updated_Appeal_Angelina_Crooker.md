## Introduction

I am writing to formally appeal all decisions made by Wareham High School that have affected my daughter, Angelina Crooker, during the 2025-2026 school year. This appeal is made pursuant to:

- The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), 20 U.S.C. § 1400 et seq.
- 34 CFR Part 300
- Massachusetts General Laws, Chapter 71B
- 603 CMR 28.00
- 603 CMR 23.00
- MGL c. 76, § 1B
- MGL c. 71, § 37H 1/2

## Decisions Being Appealed

I am appealing the following decisions made by Wareham High School:

1. The decision to deny Angelina the opportunity to take final exams.
2. The decision to deny Angelina enrollment in summer credit recovery.
3. The decision to deny Angelina participation in summer school programming.
4. The school's characterization of 18 absences as "unexcused" or "truancy."
5. The school's failure to convene an IEP Team meeting to review the absences.
6. The school's failure to consider whether absences were disability-related.
7. The school's failure to provide prior written notice for any of the above decisions.
8. The school's failure to obtain parental consent for any changes to Angelina's program.

## Factual Background

Angelina Crooker is a student with a documented disability. She has an active IEP that was in effect during the 2025-2026 school year.

Beginning in late May 2026, Angelina experienced an acute medical condition that required ongoing treatment. This condition is well-documented by her treating physician. All absences during the period in question (16-18 days in June 2026) were:

- Medically necessary
- Supported by doctor's notes
- Communicated to the school in advance
- Directly related to her disability

## Procedural Violations

The school committed at least 18 distinct procedural violations, including but not limited to:

1. Failure to convene IEP Team (34 CFR § 300.321)
2. Failure to ensure parent participation (34 CFR § 300.322)
3. Failure to provide prior written notice (34 CFR § 300.503)
4. Failure to obtain parental consent (34 CFR § 300.300)
5. Failure to consider disability-related absences
6. Failure to maintain or provide access to records
7. Failure to respond to records request
8. Failure to provide procedural safeguards notice

A complete list of all 18 violations is maintained in the case file.
