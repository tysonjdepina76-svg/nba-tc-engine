# Letter to Dean Christine Cinelli

**Date:** July 1, 2026
**From:** Sarah Crooker, parent
**To:** Christine Cinelli, Dean of Students, Wareham High School
**Re:** Your Email of June 23, 2026 — Angelina Crooker

---

Dear Ms. Cinelli,

I am in receipt of your email dated June 23, 2026 regarding my daughter, Angelina Crooker. I am writing to address the deficiencies in your correspondence and to provide you with important context that the school appears to be missing.

## Factual Corrections

### "18 Absences"

Your email states that Angelina has accumulated 18 absences. While the number is accurate, your characterization of these absences is incorrect. All 18 absences were:

- Medically necessary and supported by doctor's notes
- Communicated to the school in advance
- Directly related to Angelina's documented disability

I have maintained complete records of all medical documentation and parent communications, which I am prepared to share with the school upon request.

### "No Final Exam List"

Your email states that Angelina has been placed on the "no final exam list." This decision was made without:

- Prior written notice to me as her parent
- A meeting of the IEP Team
- Consideration of whether the absences were disability-related
- Parental consent

These omissions are not minor procedural issues. They are substantive violations of federal and state law that have denied Angelina her right to a free appropriate public education.

## Medical Documentation

Throughout the period in question, I provided the school with extensive medical documentation, including:

- Doctor's notes for each absence
- Communication with the school nurse (where applicable)
- Phone calls and emails regarding Angelina's medical situation
- In-person visits to the school

I have not received acknowledgment of any of these communications. I am now requesting that the school:

1. **Acknowledge receipt** of all medical documentation previously provided.

2. **Review the documentation** to determine which absences should be reclassified as medically excused.

3. **Engage with me** about the medical situation and its impact on Angelina's attendance.

## Requested Actions

I am requesting that you, as Dean of Students:

1. **Reverse the decision** to place Angelina on the "no final exam list."

2. **Reclassify all 18 absences** as medically excused and disability-related.

3. **Request that the IEP Team be convened** to review all decisions affecting Angelina.

4. **Provide written confirmation** of the steps the school will take to address this matter.

5. **Forward this letter to the appropriate parties**, including the principal, superintendent, and special education director.

## Procedural Safeguards

I have not received a copy of the procedural safeguards notice that the school is required to provide to parents of students with disabilities. I am requesting that this notice be provided to me immediately.

## Conclusion

I am committed to working with the school to resolve this matter. However, the school must first demonstrate a willingness to follow federal and state law. I am prepared to pursue all available remedies to protect my daughter's rights.

Sincerely,

Sarah Crooker
Parent of Angelina Crooker
