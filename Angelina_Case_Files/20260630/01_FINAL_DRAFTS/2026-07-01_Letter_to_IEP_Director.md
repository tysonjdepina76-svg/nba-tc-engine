# Letter to IEP Director / Special Education Director

**Date:** July 1, 2026
**From:** Sarah Crooker, parent
**To:** IEP Director / Special Education Director, Wareham Public Schools
**Re:** Urgent: IEP Team Meeting Request — Angelina Crooker

---

Dear IEP Director / Special Education Director,

I am writing to urgently request your intervention in a matter involving my daughter, Angelina Crooker, a Grade 9 student at Wareham High School with an active IEP.

## The Problem

Despite multiple requests, Wareham High School has refused to convene an IEP Team meeting to review decisions affecting Angelina. Specifically, the school has:

1. Denied Angelina the opportunity to take final exams
2. Denied Angelina enrollment in summer credit recovery
3. Denied Angelina participation in summer school programming
4. Refused to convene an IEP Team meeting despite multiple parent requests
5. Failed to consider whether absences were disability-related
6. Failed to provide prior written notice
7. Failed to obtain parental consent

These actions constitute clear violations of IDEA, 34 CFR Part 300, and 603 CMR 28.00.

## Why This Requires Your Immediate Attention

As the IEP Director / Special Education Director, you have oversight responsibility for ensuring that the district complies with special education law. The actions of Wareham High School are exposing the district to significant legal liability, and immediate intervention is required to:

1. **Stop ongoing harm to Angelina** — She is currently being denied educational opportunities.

2. **Prevent further violations** — Each day that passes without an IEP Team meeting is another day of non-compliance.

3. **Protect the district from liability** — The procedural violations are well-documented and could result in significant compensatory education awards if the matter proceeds to a BSEA hearing.

4. **Preserve the parent-school relationship** — Without your intervention, this matter is likely to escalate to formal complaints and litigation.

## Requested Actions

I am requesting that you take the following actions:

1. **Order the immediate convening of an IEP Team meeting** for Angelina Crooker.

2. **Ensure that the meeting is scheduled at a mutually agreeable time** for all participants, including me as the parent.

3. **Ensure that all required IEP Team members attend** the meeting, including:
   - Regular education teacher
   - Special education teacher
   - School district representative
   - Person to interpret evaluation results
   - Angelina (if appropriate)
   - Me (as parent)

4. **Ensure that prior written notice is provided** for the meeting and for any decisions made at the meeting.

5. **Review all prior communications** regarding Angelina's case to ensure that the school is fully informed of the relevant facts.

6. **Provide written confirmation** of the steps the district will take to address this matter.

## Background Information

For your reference, the relevant background is as follows:

- Angelina is a Grade 9 student with an active IEP
- Beginning in late May 2026, she experienced an acute medical condition
- All 18 absences in June 2026 were medically documented and communicated to the school
- Despite this, the school has taken adverse actions against her
- The school has not convened an IEP Team meeting despite multiple parent requests
- The school has not provided prior written notice for any decisions

## Timeline

I am requesting that the IEP Team meeting be scheduled no later than **10 business days from the date of this letter**. If the meeting is not scheduled within this timeframe, I will:

1. File a formal complaint with DESE
2. File a hearing request with BSEA
3. Contact the Massachusetts Office of the Child Advocate

## Conclusion

I trust that you will give this matter your immediate attention. I am available to discuss this case at any time.

Sincerely,

Sarah Crooker
Parent of Angelina Crooker
