# Letter to Summer School Coordinator

**Date:** July 1, 2026
**From:** Sarah Crooker, parent
**To:** Summer School Coordinator, Wareham Public Schools
**Re:** Request to Enroll Angelina Crooker in Summer Programming

---

Dear Summer School Coordinator,

I am writing to request the immediate enrollment of my daughter, Angelina Crooker, in summer school programming. Angelina is a Grade 9 student at Wareham High School with an active IEP.

## Background

Angelina was prevented from completing her coursework during the regular school year due to a documented medical condition. All 18 absences in June 2026 were medically necessary and were supported by:

- Doctor's notes for each absence
- Communication with the school in advance
- Documentation of the medical condition

Despite this, the school has refused to allow Angelina to enroll in summer credit recovery or summer school programming. This refusal is based on the school's mischaracterization of her absences as "unexcused" and its refusal to consider her medical situation.

## Why Summer School Enrollment Should Be Permitted

Summer school enrollment should be permitted for several reasons:

1. **Equitable Treatment** — Other students with medical circumstances have historically been permitted to enroll in summer programming.

2. **Compensatory Education** — Even if the original denial were valid (which it is not), Angelina is entitled to compensatory education for the procedural violations she has experienced.

3. **IEP Accommodation** — Under IDEA, summer programming may be appropriate as an accommodation for disability-related absences.

4. **District Policy** — Most district policies permit summer school enrollment for students with legitimate reasons for missing coursework.

5. **Educational Continuity** — Without summer school enrollment, Angelina will enter the 2026-2027 school year significantly behind her peers.

## Requested Action

I am requesting that you:

1. **Immediately enroll Angelina** in summer credit recovery for the courses she was unable to complete.

2. **Immediately enroll Angelina** in any other summer programming that is appropriate for her grade level.

3. **Provide written confirmation** of her enrollment, including the schedule, location, and any support services that will be provided.

4. **Coordinate with the IEP Team** to ensure that appropriate accommodations are in place.

## Procedural Background

Please be aware that the school's denial of summer school enrollment to Angelina is part of a broader pattern of procedural violations. Specifically:

- The school has not convened an IEP Team meeting
- The school has not provided prior written notice
- The school has not considered whether absences were disability-related
- The school has not obtained parental consent for any changes

These violations are well-documented and are the subject of a formal appeal dated July 1, 2026.

## Timeline

I am requesting that Angelina be enrolled in summer programming by **July 8, 2026**. If she is not enrolled by that date, I will:

1. Request that the School Committee order her enrollment
2. File a complaint with DESE
3. Request compensatory education through the BSEA process

## Conclusion

I am committed to working with the district to ensure that Angelina has every opportunity to succeed academically. I trust that you will give this request your immediate attention.

Sincerely,

Sarah Crooker
Parent of Angelina Crooker
