# Response to Principal Palladino

**Date:** July 1, 2026
**From:** Sarah Crooker, parent
**To:** Scott Palladino, Principal, Wareham High School
**Re:** Your Emails of June 24 and June 26, 2026

---

Dear Mr. Palladino,

I am in receipt of your emails dated June 24 and June 26, 2026 regarding my daughter, Angelina Crooker. After careful review, I must address several factual inaccuracies and procedural deficiencies in your correspondence.

## Factual Inaccuracies

### "18 Unexcused Absences"

Your characterization of Angelina's 18 absences as "unexcused" is factually incorrect. Every absence was:

- Medically necessary and documented by her treating physician
- Supported by a doctor's note provided to the school
- Communicated in advance via phone, email, or in-person visit

I have maintained complete records of all medical documentation and parent communications. These records demonstrate that the school was repeatedly informed of Angelina's medical situation.

### "5-Day Documentation Window"

Your reference to a "5-day documentation window" is not a policy I have been provided. I have requested a copy of this policy as part of my records request dated June 26, 2026, and have not yet received it. Without a written policy, I cannot evaluate its applicability.

Moreover, even if such a policy existed, it cannot override federal law. Under IDEA and 603 CMR 28.00, absences that are disability-related cannot be the basis for disciplinary action without first convening the IEP Team.

## Procedural Deficiencies

### Failure to Convene IEP Team

Your email states that the school "will not convene an IEP Team meeting." This is a direct violation of:

- 34 CFR § 300.321 (IEP Team composition and required meetings)
- 34 CFR § 300.322 (Parent participation)
- 34 CFR § 300.324 (Development, review, and revision of IEP)
- 603 CMR 28.00 (Massachusetts special education regulations)

The school has an affirmative obligation to convene the IEP Team when a parent's request is made or when decisions affecting the child's placement are being considered. Your refusal to do so is not discretionary.

### Failure to Provide Prior Written Notice

Your email announcing that "these decisions are final" constitutes the school's first attempt at providing notice. However, the notice is deficient under 34 CFR § 300.503 because it does not include:

- A description of the action proposed or refused
- An explanation of why the action is being taken
- A description of each evaluation procedure, assessment, record, or report used as a basis for the action
- A statement that parents have procedural safeguards
- Sources for parents to contact for assistance

### Failure to Provide Procedural Safeguards

At no point during this process has the school provided me with a copy of the procedural safeguards notice required by 34 CFR § 300.504. This is a separate and independent violation.

## Your Unavailability

I note that you will be out of the office from June 26 through July 7, 2026. This timing is concerning, as it effectively prevents meaningful engagement with the school during a critical period. I request that you ensure the Acting Principal is fully briefed on this matter and has the authority to engage substantively.

## Requested Actions

I request that the school:

1. **Reverse all decisions** affecting Angelina as set forth in my formal appeal dated July 1, 2026.
2. **Immediately convene an IEP Team meeting** upon your return.
3. **Provide complete prior written notice** for any future decisions.
4. **Provide procedural safeguards notice** to me immediately.
5. **Respond to my records request** by July 8, 2026.

## Conclusion

I remain committed to working collaboratively with the school to resolve this matter. However, the school must first demonstrate a willingness to follow federal and state law. Your current position is not legally sustainable.

Sincerely,

Parent of Angelina Crooker
