## TOPIC 1: From the Dojo to the Penitentiary
**The Decision That Changes Everything**

Every kid is a champion at something. Tyson was a world champion karate competitor—400+ trophies, international travel, Billy Blanks USA JR All-Stars. By 14, he was in DYS. By 18, adult jail. This keynote traces the exact moment a promising future became a 24-year sentence—and the decision framework he now teaches kids to avoid the same path.

## TOPIC 3: The One Decision

One decision to say "yes" to the wrong crowd. One decision to carry a gun. One decision that dominoed into 30 years of consequences. Tyson breaks down exactly how small decisions become life sentences—and how to interrupt the pattern before it starts.

**Legal Literacy for Young People**

**Audience:** High schools, community groups, parent organizations, law-related education programs

## BIO

At 8 years old, Tyson DePina was already a headline-maker. As a member of the Billy Blanks USA JR All-Stars karate team, he traveled the world—Team Canada in 1986, Trinidad and Tobago in 1987—amassing over 400 trophies. His face appeared on the sports page under the headline: *"Youngster's quiet nature belies fighting spirit."*

At 14, everything changed. After entering DYS for guns and drugs, Tyson robbed an undercover cop at gunpoint at 16. The government sought to try him as an adult. He was remanded to DYS until his 18th birthday.

From 18 to 49, Tyson cycled through county jail, state prison, and federal prison—24 years behind bars across three decades. He caught his first federal case in 2005 and a second in 2016, receiving a 96-month sentence.

Today, Tyson is channeling the same fighting spirit into a new mission: helping teenagers escape the path he walked. He is a certified jailhouse lawyer, a youth mentor, and a voice for reentry.

His message is simple: your past does not define your future. But your next decision might.
