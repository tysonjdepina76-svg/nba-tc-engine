## 5. Procedural Violations

The school committed the following distinct procedural violations:

### State Law

1. **MGL c. 76, § 1B** — Failure to provide reasonable-effort notification, meeting-and-plan for repeated absence, and meeting-and-plan for habitual absence.
2. **MGL c. 71, § 37H** — Failure to provide school-committee policy publication, due process, written notice, 10-day appeal window, right to counsel, continued educational services, and DESE data reporting before imposing a credit-penalty "removal from educational privileges."
3. **MGL c. 71, § 37H½** — Failure to provide the 5/3/5-day appeal timeline, written notice of charges, superintendent hearing, right to counsel, right to present evidence, and right to cross-examine.
4. **MGL c. 71, § 28** — Imposing a compulsory summer school requirement in a statutory framework that makes summer school voluntary.
5. **MGL c. 76, § 2 / MGL c. 119, § 21** — Applying truancy and parental-liability consequences without meeting the statutory thresholds.
6. **603 CMR 23.07** — Failing to respond to the records-amendment request within the statutory window (deadline 7/1/26).
7. **603 CMR 23.09** — Failing to schedule the Fair Hearing within 4 weeks of the 6/24/26 hearing request (deadline 7/24/26).
8. **603 CMR 23.00 (general)** — Maintaining and relying upon records reflecting procedurally invalid NC/CP designations based on unreconciled data and absent intervention.
9. **Handbook p. 43 vs. pp. 44–45** — Internal contradiction: p. 43 states there is "no differentiation between excused and unexcused absences" while pp. 44–45 operate entirely on that distinction. The handbook is void for vagueness / unenforceable as applied.
10. **Handbook p. 44** — Failure to notify parent, create an action plan, or convene a meeting on the 5th unexcused absence (10/27/25) — the "shall" language is mandatory.
11. **Handbook p. 45** — Failure to schedule the Attendance Review Committee hearing before imposing the credit penalty.

### Federal Law

12. **14th Amendment, U.S. Constitution — Procedural Due Process** (*Goss v. Lopez*, 419 U.S. 565 (1975)). A credit designation on a transcript is a property interest in educational benefits. The school provided neither notice nor an opportunity to be heard before imposing the penalty.
13. **FERPA, 34 CFR § 99.10** — Records used to impose penalties were never disclosed to the parent before penalty imposition.
14. **FERPA, 34 CFR § 99.20** — Records-amendment request submitted 6/24/26; statutory clock running.
15. **FERPA, 34 CFR § 99.21** — No hearing offered; no opportunity to place a statement of disagreement in the record.
16. **FERPA, 34 CFR § 99.7** — No annual notification of FERPA rights in the context of the pending penalties.
17. **IDEA, 34 CFR § 300.503 — Prior Written Notice.** The school changed Angelina's placement (summer school / NC-CP) without providing the 10-day prior written notice the IDEA requires. The June 26, 2026 progress report, issued three days after the penalty, made no reference to the placement change — direct documentary evidence that no PWN was issued through normal channels.
18. **IDEA, 34 CFR § 300.300(d)(3) — Parental Consent.** No parental consent was obtained for the change in placement.
19. **IDEA, 34 CFR §§ 300.321, 300.322 — IEP Team / Parent Participation.** No IEP Team meeting was convened; the parent was not afforded the opportunity to participate in any placement decision affecting Angelina, despite her active IEP.
20. **IDEA, 34 CFR § 300.501 — Procedural Safeguards Notice.** Procedural safeguards notice was not provided to the parent in connection with the placement change.
21. **IDEA, 34 CFR § 300.324(a)(2)(i) — Behavior Intervention Plan.** The school's awareness of bullying and the disability-related attendance pattern triggered the obligation to consider and develop a behavior intervention plan. None was developed.

## 6. Argument — The Penalty Is the Direct Product of the School's Own Procedural Failure

The only reason Angelina reached 9 total absences is that the school suppressed the 10/27/25 intervention required by Handbook p. 44 and MGL c. 76 § 1B. **For 239 days, the school did nothing — and then penalized the student for the condition its own silence created.** Equity will not permit a party to benefit from its own procedural failure. The school cannot create the condition it then penalizes and call the penalty "within policy."

## 7. Argument — The Handbook Is Void for Vagueness / Unenforceable as Applied

Page 43 declares that there is no differentiation between excused and unexcused absences. Pages 44 and 45 rely entirely on that distinction. The school cannot simultaneously claim the distinction does not exist and rely on it. Ambiguity in school-imposed rules is construed against the school. The published policy does not authorize the penalty the school seeks to impose.

## 8. Argument — The School Exceeded Its Statutory Authority

The Massachusetts Legislature has drawn specific lines for truancy (8 unexcused/quarter — § 21), parental liability (7+ unexcused in 6 months — § 2), and chronic absenteeism (10% of 180 days — DESE). Angelina meets none of these thresholds. The school cannot use its internal handbook to impose a consequence the legislature did not authorize.

## 9. Argument — The 14-Day Data Gap Must Be Reconciled Before Any Penalty Is Valid

PowerSchool (the system of record) and Dean Cinelli's 6/23/26 email both show 16 absences. The school's separate 6/23/26 communication claims 30 days / 48 periods. **The 14-day gap is unexplained.** The burden of proof on data accuracy is on the school, not on the parent. Until the school reconciles the gap, no penalty premised on the higher figure can stand. At 16 days, the chronic-absenteeism argument is decisive (8.9% < 10%).

## 10. Argument — The Active IEP Required Team-Based Decision-Making

Because Angelina has an active IEP, the building administration did not have unilateral authority to change her placement. The change required an IEP Team meeting with all required participants (34 CFR § 300.321), prior written notice to the parent (34 CFR § 300.503), and parental consent (34 CFR § 300.300). None of these steps occurred. The June 26, 2026 progress report confirms that the placement change was not communicated through the normal IEP/progress-reporting channel, and the penalty was therefore imposed outside the IEP framework altogether.
