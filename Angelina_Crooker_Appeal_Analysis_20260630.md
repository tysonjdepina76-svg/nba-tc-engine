## PART 5 — STRONGEST LEGAL ARGUMENTS

### 1. VOID-FOR-PROCEDURE (STRONGEST)
The handbook's "shall" language at p.44 is mandatory. 8 months of silence after the Oct 27, 2025 trigger is an admitted, documented breach. A penalty imposed without satisfying pre-conditions is procedurally void. This is the spine of the case.

### 2. UNREBUTTED EMAIL EVIDENCE
Gmail search across 3 family accounts (parent, sister, grandmother) = zero attendance communications. School has no documentation of any intervention attempt. They cannot rebut what was never sent. School has demonstrably contacted all 3 contacts on other matters in the last 3 months, proving contact info is current and functional.

### 3. THRESHOLD MISMATCH (DISPOSITIVE ON LABELS)
- Not habitually truant (MGL Ch. 119 §21) — fails 8/quarter
- Not chronically absent (DESE) — 8.9% < 10%
- Not over parental liability threshold (MGL Ch. 76 §2) — 7/6mo not clearly exceeded
- Not at 20-unexcused credit retrieval bar (p.45) — 11 unexcused
The school can only reach NC by counting ALL absences, contradicting their own p.44 framework.

### 4. INTERNAL HANDBOOK CONTRADICTION
p.43–44 require excused/unexcused distinction. p.45 NC clause ignores it. School cannot selectively apply the distinction only when it hurts the student. Ambiguity in school-imposed rules is construed against the school (standard MA contract/regulatory interpretation default).

### 5. STUDENT'S OWN PERFORMANCE NEGATES "DISENGAGEMENT" RATIONALE
B+ avg, honor roll x2, clean discipline, bullying victim, Q4 improvement to 1 absence. The attendance policy targets disengaged students. Angelina is not disengaged. The policy's stated purpose is not served by applying it to her.

### 6. 14TH AMENDMENT + 603 CMR 23.00 BACKSTOP
NC/CP designations on a transcript are a "property" interest requiring due process. The 603 CMR 23.00 records challenge is independent of the appeal and survives summer break with statutory deadlines.

## PART 6 — ANTICIPATED WEAKNESSES / COUNTERS

- **Substantive seriousness of 16 absences** — school will argue the outcome is fair regardless of procedure. *Counter:* The handbook's own design makes process the gate. If process doesn't matter, the p.44 "shall" language is meaningless surplusage.
- **"Reasonable effort to meet" gives school latitude** — school may claim it tried. *Counter:* Gmail evidence + zero documentation of any attempt. The school bears the burden of proof on "reasonable effort" and has no record.
- **Parent's concession of partial responsibility** — school may use this to muddy the procedural default. *Counter:* Parent explicitly frames her concession as separate from the procedural void. Both can be true: parent failed AND school failed; only one is the legal basis for the penalty.
