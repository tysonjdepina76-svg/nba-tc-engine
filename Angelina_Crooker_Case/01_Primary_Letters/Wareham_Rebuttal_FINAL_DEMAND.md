### Category 2: The School Failed to Provide Mandatory Intervention — 3 Counts

**Violation 7 — October 27, 2025:** Page 44, word for word: "Students will be required to develop an action plan for student's attendance with the Principal or designee, Guidance Counselor and their parents/guardians." No action plan was created. No parent was contacted. No meeting was held.

**Violation 8 — October 27, 2025:** Page 44, word for word: "The Principal or designee, or Guidance Counselor shall make a reasonable effort to meet with the parent." No reasonable effort was made. No effort of any kind was made.

**Violation 9 — January 2026 through June 2026:** After reaching 7, 8, 9, 10, and 11 unexcused absences (Jan 8 through Mar 17, 2026), the school's obligation to intervene intensified. The silence continued through the end of the school year.

### Category 3: The Handbook Contradicts Itself — 4 Counts

**Violation 10 — Page 43 vs. Page 44:** Page 43 declares "there is no differentiation between excused and unexcused absences." Page 44 applies every notification and intervention trigger ONLY to unexcused absences. The school cannot claim both simultaneously.

**Violation 11 — Page 43 vs. Page 45:** Page 43 says no differentiation. Page 45 bars credit retrieval at 20+ UNEXCUSED absences. If there is no differentiation, the 20-unexcused rule is meaningless. The school relies on a distinction it claims does not exist.

**Violation 12 — Page 44 vs. Page 45:** Page 44 requires intervention at 5 unexcused absences. Page 45 imposes NC at 9 total absences regardless of excused/unexcused status. A student can receive NC without ever triggering the intervention the policy promises. The system is designed to skip the safety net.

**Violation 13 — Tardies:** PowerSchool records 27 tardies. The handbook's credit/attendance policy (Pages 42-45) contains zero mention of tardies in the CP, NC, credit retrieval, or intervention framework. If tardies factored into Angelina's outcome, the school applied an unwritten standard. If they did not, they are irrelevant — and this is confirmed.

### Category 4: Angelina Does Not Meet Any Legal Threshold — 3 Counts

**Violation 14 — MGL Chapter 119, §21 (Habitual Truancy):** Requires 8+ unexcused absences per quarter. Q1=5, Q2=2, Q3=4, Q4=0. Angelina is not a habitual truant under Massachusetts law in any quarter. Full stop.

**Violation 15 — MGL Chapter 76, §2 (Parent Criminal Liability):** Requires 7+ unexcused full school days in a six-month period. Semester 1 = 7 (borderline). Semester 2 = 4. The school never raised this because it cannot.

**Violation 16 — DESE Chronic Absenteeism (10% Rule):** 10% of 180 school days = 18 days. Angelina has 16 days. 8.9%. She is not chronically absent under DESE standards. Period.

All require intervention before escalation. The school skipped every required step and imposed the penalty at the endpoint. This is a sentence without a trial. The penalty is void.

Any NC/CP designation based on unreconciled data, imposed without the mandatory intervention process, is procedurally invalid. The parent hereby challenges the record. The principal has one week to respond. If denied: appeal to the Superintendent (two weeks). If denied: Fair Hearing before the School Committee (within four weeks). All deadlines will be enforced.
