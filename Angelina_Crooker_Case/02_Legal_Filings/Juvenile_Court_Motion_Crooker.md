## III. LEGAL ARGUMENT

### A. Angelina Crooker Is Not a Habitual Truant Under MGL Ch. 119, § 21

Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 119, § 21 defines a "habitual truant" as a student who willfully fails to attend school for more than **8 school days in a quarter**.

| Quarter | Unexcused Absences | Meets 8-Day Threshold? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Q1 | 5 | **NO** |
| Q2 | 2 | **NO** |
| Q3 | 4 | **NO** |
| Q4 | 0 | **NO** |

Angelina Crooker does not meet the statutory definition of habitual truancy in any quarter. She is not a habitual truant under Massachusetts law. There is no legal basis for a CRA petition.

### B. The School Failed to Satisfy MGL Ch. 119, § 21 Pre-Filing Requirements

Even if Angelina were a habitual truant — which she is not — MGL Chapter 119, § 21 requires that before filing a CRA petition, the school must:

1. Notify the parent of the student's absences
2. Make a reasonable effort to meet with the parent
3. Develop and attempt interventions to address the attendance issues

The school has done none of these things. A CRA petition filed without satisfying these prerequisites is procedurally defective and should be dismissed.

### C. The School's Own Handbook Requires Pre-Intervention — And Was Ignored

The Wareham High School Handbook, Page 44, establishes an intervention process that mirrors state law. The school drafted this policy. The school published it to families. The school is bound by it. The school ignored it entirely.

A school that cannot follow its own mandatory pre-penalty procedures cannot ask a court to enforce the penalties.

### D. DESE Chronic Absenteeism Threshold Is Not Met

DESE defines chronic absenteeism as 10% or more of school days missed. 10% of 180 days = 18 days. Angelina has 16 total absences (8.9%). She is below the state definition of chronic absenteeism.

### E. The Penalty Exists Only Because the School Suppressed the Intervention

The only reason Angelina reached 9+ total absences — which triggers NC under Page 45 — is that the school failed to intervene at 5 unexcused absences on October 27, 2025. Had the school followed its own policy:

- The parent would have been notified
- An action plan would have been created with the parent, student, Principal, and Guidance Counselor
- Interventions would have been attempted
- Angelina's Q2-Q4 attendance (9 total absences, only 6 unexcused) might have been different

The school cannot leverage its own procedural failures into a basis for court involvement.
