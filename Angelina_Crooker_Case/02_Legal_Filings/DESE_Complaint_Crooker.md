PowerSchool Legend: A = Absent Unexcused, X = Absent Excused, Blank = Present. Confirmed by Dean Christine Cinelli via email, June 23, 2026.

### B. The Records Are Inaccurate Because:

1. **The NC/CP designation is based on attendance data accumulated without the mandatory intervention the handbook requires.** Under Wareham High School Handbook Page 44, when a student reaches 5 unexcused absences, the school MUST notify the parent, create an action plan with the parent and student, and make a reasonable effort to meet. Angelina reached her 5th unexcused absence on October 27, 2025. None of the three required actions occurred.

2. **The handbook contradicts itself on whether excused and unexcused absences count the same.** Page 43 states: "For purposes of this policy and meeting the guidelines set by the state, there is no differentiation between excused and unexcused absences." Yet Pages 44-45 rely entirely on the excused/unexcused distinction for intervention triggers and credit retrieval bars. The records cannot be accurate when the governing policy cannot be consistently applied.

3. **The total absence count (16) used for NC/CP is below multiple state thresholds.**

   - MGL Ch. 119 §21 (Habitual Truancy): 8+ unexcused per quarter — Angelina never reached this (Q1=5, Q2=2, Q3=4, Q4=0)
   - DESE Chronic Absenteeism: 18 days (10% of 180) — Angelina has 16 (8.9%)
   - The school classified Angelina under consequences that state law does not support.

## IV. PROCEDURAL VIOLATIONS

### A. Failure to Notify (Handbook Page 44)

The school failed to notify the parent at every checkpoint its own policy requires:

1. **2+ unexcused class periods over 5 days** — Reached \~September 11, 2025. No notification.
2. **5+ unexcused days in a school year** — Reached October 27, 2025. No notification.
3. **Any absence without parent contact within 3 days** — Multiple dates. No notification.
4. **5th unexcused absence — mandatory notification** — October 27, 2025. No notification.

**Confirmed:** A search of all family Gmail accounts shows zero emails from Wareham High School regarding Angelina's attendance for the entire 2025-2026 school year.

### B. Failure to Intervene (Handbook Page 44)

On October 27, 2025 — the date Angelina reached her 5th unexcused absence — the handbook requires three mandatory actions. Word for word:

> "Upon reaching the fifth unexcused absence in a school year, parents/guardians **will** be notified. Students **will be required to develop an action plan** for student's attendance with the Principal or designee, Guidance Counselor and their parents/guardians. The Principal or designee, or Guidance Counselor **shall make a reasonable effort to meet** with the parent or guardian."

**None of these three actions were taken.** Not on October 27, 2025. Not in the 8 months that followed. Not ever.

### C. Penalty Imposed Without Due Process

At the end of the 2025-2026 school year — on or about June 23, 2026 — the school imposed a summer school requirement and/or NC/CP designation on Angelina Crooker. This penalty was imposed:

- Without any prior notification to the parent
- Without the mandatory action plan
- Without the mandatory meeting
- Without the Attendance Review Committee hearing required by Page 45
- Without any communication from the school about Angelina's attendance for the entire school year

This constitutes a violation of procedural due process under the 14th Amendment to the U.S. Constitution, Massachusetts education law, and DESE policy.

## V. HANDBOOK SELF-CONTRADICTIONS (Policy Is Unenforceable)

The Wareham High School Handbook contradicts itself on the most fundamental question of its attendance policy:

| Contradiction | Pages |
| --- | --- |
| "No differentiation between excused and unexcused absences" | Page 43 |
| Every intervention trigger applies ONLY to unexcused absences | Page 44 |
| 20-unexcused credit retrieval bar assumes differentiation exists | Page 45 |
| NC imposed at 9 total days regardless of excused/unexcused | Page 45 |

A policy that cannot state consistently whether excused and unexcused absences are the same or different cannot be enforced. Under 603 CMR 23.07, a student record based on an internally contradictory policy is an inaccurate record.
